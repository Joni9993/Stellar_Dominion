# RULES OF THE GALAXY
### Stellar Dominion — Player's Handbook

*Everything you need to know to play, plan, and dominate.*

---

## THE GALAXY AT A GLANCE

Stellar Dominion is a turn-based multiplayer strategy game for **2–6 commanders**. Each player controls the flagship of an alien faction and travels through a galaxy of **18 star systems**. You earn credits through trade, spend them on ship parts and fuel, and hunt down powerful **Artifacts**. The first commander to collect enough Artifacts wins — or, if 20 cycles pass, whoever holds the most.

**The three pillars of the game: COMBAT · TRADE · ARTIFACTS**

---

## QUICK STRATEGY TIPS

- **Watch the Rumor.** It pulls all commanders toward one spot. Timing your approach (or avoiding it entirely) is often the key decision of a cycle.
- **Read enemy builds.** Every ship loadout is visible on the map. If an enemy runs heavy shields, bring Railguns. If they run Armor, bring Lasers.
- **Artifacts compete for slots.** Holding 3 Artifacts sounds powerful — and it is — but that's 3 fewer weapons or shields. Fragile Artifact-heavy builds can be punished by lean combat ships.
- **Defeat hurts, but isn't fatal.** A defeated ship warps home instantly and loses something to the Boarding Roulette — but can jump again next turn. You might only lose credits. Press your advantage before they recover.
- **Trade early, fight late — or the reverse.** Snowball credits fast enough to buy Artifacts through the Rumor system. Or threaten other commanders early before they can build up.

---

## THE GALAXY MAP

The galaxy contains **18 systems** connected by **jump lanes**. The map is always the same shape, but system contents (goods, fuel prices) vary each game. The galaxy is divided into **4 regions**, each guaranteed to have at least one fuel source and one tradeable good.

### Systems
- Some systems have a **Station** — you can trade goods, buy ship parts, hire crew, and refuel there.
- Systems without stations are waypoints.
- Every player's ship is **always visible** to everyone. There is no fog of war.

### Inspecting Other Commanders
When you select a system and see enemy commanders listed under **COMMANDERS HERE**, tap any entry to open a full overview of that commander: their ship build (3×3 grid), combat stats, resources (credits and fuel), equipped artifacts, and crew. Use this to plan your fights — know their weapons before you attack.

### Jump Travel
- Each turn you may jump **once per point of Jump Range** on your ship (default: 1).
- A standard ship can jump to one adjacent system per turn. Equipping Ion Engines or Fuel Tanks increases your Jump Range, letting you jump multiple times in a single turn.
- Each individual jump costs **fuel** (8–16 units depending on lane distance). Fuel costs are the same regardless of how many jumps you make.
- If you run out of fuel mid-turn, you cannot jump again that turn.

### Emergency Signal
If you don't have enough fuel to reach **any** neighboring system, you are stranded. When you select your own system on the map, a red **⚠ EMERGENCY SIGNAL** button appears in place of the normal movement options.

- Press it to **immediately teleport back to your home system**.
- Your fuel is restored to **40 units**.
- Your **turn ends immediately** — you get no further actions that round.

The emergency signal is always available if you are truly stranded. It cannot be used as a shortcut when you still have enough fuel to jump somewhere.

## TURNS & PHASES

Players take turns in order. One cycle = all players have taken their turn once.

**On your turn you do two things:**

**1. MOVE (optional)**
Jump to adjacent systems. Your **Jump Range** stat determines how many times you can jump in a single turn (default: 1). You may use all jumps, some, or none — staying put is always valid. Each jump costs fuel.

**2. ACTION (pick exactly one)**
- **STATION** — At a station: buy/sell goods, buy/sell modules (MODULES tab), hire crew, refuel, change your ship build.
- **ATTACK** — If an enemy ship is in your current system, you can attack. If multiple enemies are present, a target selection window opens — pick who to fight. A combat auto-battle plays out. After the fight, the winner's boarding crew searches the loser's ship — see **Boarding Roulette** below.
- **CLAIM ARTIFACT** — If the Rumored Artifact is in your current system, you can buy it from the smuggler (if you can afford it).

**3. END TURN**
Your turn ends. The next player goes.

After all players have taken their turn, the **cycle counter** advances and every player receives **50 credits** as basic income. A new Rumored Artifact may spawn.

## RESOURCES

| Resource | Symbol | How you get it | What it's for |
|---|---|---|---|
| Credits | ◈ | Trading goods, cycle income | Buy parts, fuel, crew, claim Artifacts |
| Fuel | ⛽ | Buy at stations, some systems | Jump between systems |
| Artifacts | ✦ | Claim from smuggler, win in combat | Victory condition + combat power |
| Cargo | — | Buy goods at low price, sell high | Generating credits |

**Starting values:** 500 ◈ credits, 60 fuel (max 100), 10 base cargo capacity (expand with Cargo Bay modules, +30 each).

## ECONOMY & TRADE

Every station system has one **trade good** (Ore, Data, Tech, or Fuel cells). Goods have a buy price and a sell price — the spread is your profit.

The key to trading is **price gradients**: a good that is cheap in one region is expensive in another. Plan your route around these differences.

**Primary goods** (each region's export specialty) are cheap to buy (8–16◈) but sell back for almost nothing (3–7◈) at the same station — the station is already oversupplied. Profit comes from carrying them to another region. Primary goods replenish **+3 units every cycle**. Fuel also replenishes **+5 units every cycle** at stations. Secondary imported goods are expensive (45–65◈ to buy) but a station will pay 30–50◈ when you bring them in.

Build your cargo hold (Cargo Bay parts) to carry more goods per trip.

**Sell price bonus:** Hiring a **Trader** crew member adds ×1.20 to all sell revenue. The Station shows the effective price you will actually receive — look for the multiplier indicator next to the sell price.

## THE ARTIFACT SYSTEM

Artifacts are the heart of the game. They serve two purposes:
1. **Victory condition** — collect X Artifacts to win instantly (threshold depends on player count).
2. **Combat power** — each Artifact may occupy a grid slot on your ship and has a powerful combat ability.

### Faction Artifacts
Every commander starts with one Artifact that defines their faction's identity. You always start with it — but you can lose it in combat.

### The Rumor System (Mario Party Star)
At all times, **exactly one neutral Artifact** is available on the map. Everyone can see it — its location is announced as a galaxy-wide **RUMOR ping**.

**How claiming works:**
1. The Artifact is held by a smuggler at a specific system.
2. You must fly to that system and pay the listed price in credits.
3. Once claimed, the Rumor goes quiet. A new Artifact will spawn in a random system after the last one was claimed.

**Prices rise over time:** Early Artifacts cost ~300 ◈. The price increases by 50 ◈ for each Artifact already claimed in the game.

**The strategic tension:** Everyone sees the same Rumor. Do you race there (risking a fight and spending credits), or spend these cycles trading and upgrading, then strike later? Commanders with many Artifacts will be hunted at the Rumor site.

### Boarding Roulette (Combat Loot)
After every combat, the winner's boarding crew searches the defeated ship. The outcome is determined randomly — both players see an animated roulette reveal:

| Outcome | Chance | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **Artifact** | 15% | Winner takes one random Artifact from the loser |
| **Module** | 35% | Winner takes one random installed or inventory module from the loser (goes to winner's inventory) |
| **Credits** | 50% | Winner takes 15% of the loser's current credit balance |

**Fallback:** If the rolled outcome is unavailable (e.g., "Artifact" but loser has none, or "Module" but loser has an empty build), the result automatically falls back to Credits.

The **loser's ship warps back to their home system** immediately and can jump again on their very next turn.

---

## WIN CONDITIONS

- **Instant win:** Be the first to collect X Artifacts simultaneously.
  - 2 players → 3 Artifacts
  - 3–4 players → 4 Artifacts
  - 5–6 players → 5 Artifacts
- **Cycle limit win:** After 20 cycles, the commander with the most Artifacts wins.
- Ties at cycle end: the player who claimed their last Artifact earliest wins.

## THE 6 FACTIONS

Each faction has a distinct **ship silhouette** (visible in the Shipyard and in Combat), a **color**, a different strength profile across Combat / Trade / Artifacts, and a unique **starting Artifact** that defines their combat identity.

---

### Vol'Kesh Combine
*Reptiloid merchant guild. Opulent, golden freighters with a wide, bulging cargo hull.*
**Color:** Gold `#d9a441`
**Strengths:** Trade-oriented playstyle — use credits from trading to claim Rumored Artifacts early and often.
**Weaknesses:** No inherent combat stat advantage; the Gilded Aegis is your main defensive tool.
**Starting Artifact — Gilded Aegis:** Every 6 seconds (300 ticks), restores up to 30 shield points. Works without a Shield Projector — the Aegis generates its own 30-point temporary shield pool. With a Shield Projector equipped, restores toward your normal cap instead.

---

### Korthaar Clans
*Warrior caste. Brutalist armored warships with a heavy wedge, forward weapon booms, and aggressive spiked bow.*
**Color:** Blood Red `#c4513b`
**Strengths:** Combat — the Wrath Engine turns losing fights into comebacks; best for aggressive, early-fight strategies.
**Weaknesses:** Trade — a pure combat faction; profits come from winning fights, not market runs.
**Starting Artifact — Wrath Engine:** When hull drops below 50%, fire rate increases by +50%.

---

### Idryn Concord
*High culture. Sleek, luminous ships with elegant swept wings and glowing edge accents.*
**Color:** Teal `#5fa8a4`
**Strengths:** Artifacts — the Concord Prism amplifies every other Artifact you hold by ×1.25; the more Artifacts you collect, the stronger you become.
**Weaknesses:** Average combat firepower with no special damage or defense bonus; must rely on Artifact stacking for power.
**Starting Artifact — Concord Prism:** All other Artifacts on your ship deal +25% effectiveness.

---

### Nyxari Nomads
*Explorers and scouts. Slender ships with long sensor antennae and thin solar sail wings.*
**Color:** Olive Green `#8a9a5b`
**Strengths:** Mobility — Far Sight grants a first-strike advantage and extra evasion; position to reach the Rumor before rivals.
**Weaknesses:** No special economic or combat stat advantage; relies entirely on positioning and timing.
**Starting Artifact — Far Sight:** All your weapons fire at half their normal cooldown on the first shot (First Strike — you fire before enemies in an even matchup). Grants +15% permanent evasion.

---

### The Rask
*Scavenger swarm. Asymmetric patchwork raiders built from stolen and salvaged parts.*
**Color:** Signal Orange `#e8512e`
**Strengths:** Disruption — the Weapon Jammer shuts down an enemy weapon when cornered, giving you a fighting chance to turn losing fights around.
**Weaknesses:** No special stat advantage; success depends on building a strong combat loadout and picking good fights.
**Starting Artifact — Weapon Jammer:** When your own hull drops below 30%, instantly disables one random enemy weapon for 4 seconds (200 ticks). Triggers once per combat.

---

### Vaesh Synod
*Synthetic AI collective. Crystalline geometric vessels with perfect angular symmetry and pulsing circuitry.*
**Color:** Purple `#8a7caa`
**Strengths:** Technology — exclusive access to **Advanced Reactor** and **Advanced Capacitor** modules, providing unmatched energy generation for sustained weapon output.
**Weaknesses:** Expensive to maintain and upgrade; early-game credits must go toward upgrading the energy supply.
**Starting Artifact — Overclock Matrix:** The next 3 weapon shots ignore enemy shields entirely.

#### Vaesh Exclusive Modules
| Module | Type | Combat Effect | Cost |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Advanced Reactor** | Generator | +14 energy/tick, +50 max energy | 220◈ |
| **Advanced Capacitor** | Capacitor | +6 energy/tick, +60 max energy | 200◈ |

These modules are sold **only at Dustfall** (Vaesh home system) and only commanders playing as Vaesh can purchase them. They appear in the MODULES tab at Dustfall alongside the 3 standard modules offered there. When owned, they show in the Shipyard inventory palette marked as **VAESH TECH**.

## YOUR SHIP: THE 3×3 GRID

Your ship has **9 module slots** arranged in a 3×3 grid. This is your build — your combat loadout, your utility tools, your Artifacts. **Every decision costs you somewhere else.**

You can only change your build **at a station, on your turn**.

### Starting Modules
Every faction starts with 3 modules pre-equipped in the grid (one Artifact + two other modules). These starter modules are yours — you own them. Remove them from the grid and they go into your **module inventory**; equip them from inventory back into the grid at any time.

### Buying Modules
At any station, open the **MODULES** tab to see that station's 3 available modules for sale. Modules are unlimited stock — buy as many copies as you want if you have the credits. Bought modules go directly to your inventory.

### Module Inventory
Modules you own but haven't placed in the grid live in your **inventory**, visible in the left palette of the Shipyard. Drag any module from the palette into a grid slot to equip it. Dragging from the palette onto a slot that is already occupied displaces the old module back to inventory automatically.

You can own **duplicates** — nine Pulse Lasers if you want them. There is no limit.

### Selling Modules
Open the MODULES tab at any station and scroll to **YOUR INVENTORY** to sell owned modules back for **50% of their original cost**. Modules currently equipped in the grid must first be removed (via the Shipyard) before they can be sold.

### The Three Zones

The grid is divided into three horizontal rows, each with different bonuses:

| Row | Zone | Bonus |
|---|---|---|
| Top (slots 1–3) | **BOW** | Weapons here gain **+10% fire rate** and **+10% accuracy** in combat |
| Middle (slots 4–6) | **CORE** | No direct zone bonus — but the adjacency hub for energy modules (see below) |
| Bottom (slots 7–9) | **STERN** | Recommended zone for engines and utility modules; no additional zone bonus |

### Adjacency Bonuses
Certain module combinations create a bonus when placed **next to each other** in the grid. Only the weapon benefits and lights up with a **+FR** badge — the energy module does not:

| Weapon | Benefits from | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **Pulse Laser** | Adjacent **Reactor** | −15% cooldown (fires faster) |
| **Railgun** | Adjacent **Capacitor** | −15% cooldown (fires faster) |
| **Missile Pod** | Adjacent **Cargo Bay** | −15% cooldown (more ammo space = faster reload) |

The **Shield Projector** also benefits from adjacency: when placed next to any Reactor or Capacitor, its shield regen increases from **0.33 → 0.5 per tick**.

### Your Crew (separate from the grid)
Two crew slots sit at the **nose of the ship**, outside the 9-slot grid. You can hire and swap crew at stations. Crew bonuses stack with faction passives.

| Crew | Hire Cost | Active Bonus |
|---|---|---|
| Gunner | 120◈ | +15% accuracy on all weapons in combat |
| Engineer | 100◈ | +3 energy per tick in combat (helps sustain 2–3 weapons without stalling) |
| Smuggler | 90◈ | −3 fuel per jump (minimum 1 fuel) |
| Trader | 110◈ | +20% sell revenue on all goods |
| Demolitions Expert | 130◈ | Missile Pod damage ×1.5 in combat (5 → 7.5, rounded to 8 per hit) |

| Module | Damage Type | Character | Best Zone |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pulse Laser | Laser | Fast, low damage, great vs shields | Bow |

### Defenses
| Module | Type | What it does | Best Zone |
|---|---|---|---|
| Shield Projector | Shield | Energy pool that regenerates between volleys | Core |
| Armor Plate | Armor | Flat damage reduction on every hit | Core |
| Point-Defense | Point-Defense | Intercepts and destroys incoming missiles | Bow/Core |

### Utility & Power
| Module | What it does | Best Zone |
|---|---|---|
| Reactor | +8 energy/tick · +40 max energy. A **Pulse Laser** placed next to it gains −15% cooldown (the Laser fires faster; the Reactor does not glow). | Core |
| Capacitor | +4 energy/tick · +20 max energy. A **Railgun** placed next to it gains −15% cooldown (the Railgun fires faster; the Capacitor does not glow). | Core |

### Artifacts
Artifacts sit in a **Core or any slot** and provide a powerful active combat ability. They count toward your victory total. Having many is powerful — but each one takes a slot away from weapons and shields.

## COMBAT

When two ships meet and one attacks, an **auto-battle** plays out. You watch ~10–15 seconds of automated combat. Builds are set before the fight — there is no input during combat.

Your build is **always visible to other players**. Inspect enemy ships on the map before you commit to a fight. Build to counter what you see.

### Rock-Paper-Scissors: The Counter System

Three weapon types, three defense types. Each weapon is strong against one defense and weak against another.

| Weapon | Bonus damage | Penalty damage (×0.6) |
|---|---|---|
| **Laser** | vs Shield **×2.0** | vs Armor |
| **Railgun (Kinetic)** | vs Armor ×1.5 | vs Shield |
| **Missile** | Bypasses shields entirely, full hull damage | Intercepted by Point-Defense |

**The triangle:** Laser beats Shield → Railgun beats Armor → Armor beats Laser
**Missile is the wild card:** devastating but hard countered by Point-Defense.

### Weapon Stats

| Weapon | Damage/hit | Accuracy | Cooldown | Energy Cost | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Pulse Laser** | 4 | 85% | 14 ticks | 8 | Fast, great vs shields |
| **Railgun** | 8 | 72% | 22 ticks | 20 | Slow, devastating vs armor |
| **Missile Pod** | 5 | 90% | 20 ticks | 12 | Bypasses shields; point-def can intercept |

**Accuracy** is reduced by the enemy's **evasion** (Ion Engines and Far Sight grant evasion). Weapons placed in the **BOW zone** gain +10% accuracy.

**Adjacency bonus:** A Reactor or Capacitor placed next to a weapon reduces that weapon's cooldown by 15% (faster firing).

### How Combat Resolves

The combat engine runs a **tick simulation** at 50 ticks per second (simulation time), up to 1,500 ticks maximum (~30 seconds of fight). Each tick the following happens in order:

1. **Energy regenerates** — every ship gains energy each tick (see Energy below).
2. **Shields regenerate** — shield pool refills by the ship's shield-regen value per tick.
3. **Artifact effects trigger** — cooldown-based artifacts count down and fire.
4. **Weapons fire** — each weapon counts down its cooldown independently. When cooldown hits zero *and* there is enough energy, the weapon fires and its cooldown resets. If there is not enough energy, the weapon stays at zero and waits until energy is available.
5. **Hit roll** — weapon accuracy × (1 − enemy evasion). If failed: miss, no damage.
6. **Missile intercept check** — before a missile hits, Point-Defense gets its intercept roll (50% chance).
7. **Phase Drive dodge** — if Phase Drive's dodge window is active (1s every 6s), each incoming attack has a 50% chance to be evaded entirely.
8. **Damage applied** — shields absorb damage first; overflow carries to hull. Armor reduces all hull hits by a flat 6.
9. **First ship to 0 hull loses.** Time limit reached → higher hull% wins.

### Energy System

Every ship has an energy pool that powers its weapons. Without energy a weapon cannot fire.

| Value | Base (no generator) | With Reactor | With Capacitor |
|---|---|---|---|
| Energy/tick | 1 | +8 (total 9) | +4 (total 5) |
| Max energy | 30 | +40 (total 70) | +20 (total 50) |
| Starting energy | 10 | 10 | 10 |

**Why this matters:** With only 1 energy/tick, a Railgun (cost 20) takes 20 ticks to fully recharge. A Pulse Laser (cost 8) takes 8 ticks. Without a Reactor or Capacitor, running two heavy weapons simultaneously will drain your energy pool and force weapons to wait — they stay charged but idle until enough energy builds up. A Reactor eliminates this bottleneck entirely.

**Rule of thumb:** One weapon on a bare ship is fine. Two or more weapons without a generator will regularly stall. Three weapons without a generator will barely function.

The Adjacency Bonus (Reactor/Capacitor next to a Weapon) also reduces that weapon's cooldown by 15%, letting it fire faster.

**Part stats for combat:**

| Part | Combat effect |
|---|---|
| Shield Projector | +60 shield pool, +0.33 shield/tick regen (→ 0.5/tick when adjacent to Reactor or Capacitor) |
| Armor Plate | +40 hull, flat 6 damage reduction per hit |
| Point-Defense | 50% chance to intercept each incoming missile |
| Reactor | +8 energy/tick, +40 energy maximum |
| Capacitor | +4 energy/tick, +20 energy maximum |
| Ion Engine | +10% evasion |

**Faction artifact effects in combat:**

| Artifact | Owner | Trigger | Effect |
|---|---|---|---|
| Gilded Aegis | Vol'Kesh | Every 6s (300 ticks) | Restores up to 30 shield. Works without a Shield Projector (own 30-point pool); with one equipped, restores toward your normal cap. |
| Wrath Engine | Korthaar | Own hull drops below 50% | All weapon cooldowns permanently −33% (fire rate +50%). One-time, stays active. |
| Concord Prism | Idryn | Passive | All other equipped artifacts +25% effectiveness (cooldowns shorter, durations longer, values higher) |
| Far Sight | Nyxari | Combat start | All weapons fire at half cooldown on first shot (First Strike); +15% permanent evasion |
| Weapon Jammer | The Rask | Own hull drops below 30% | Disables one random enemy weapon for 4s (200 ticks). One-time per combat. |
| Overclock Matrix | Vaesh | Combat start | First 3 shots bypass enemy shields entirely (laser/railgun only; missiles always bypass shields) |

**After combat:** The Boarding Roulette determines what the winner takes (15% Artifact · 35% Module · 50% Credits). See the **Boarding Roulette** section above for full details.
The **loser's ship warps back to their home system** immediately. They can jump again on their very next turn.

## NEUTRAL ARTIFACTS (THE RUMOR POOL)

These six neutral Artifacts cycle through the game one at a time as the Rumored Artifact. They are **intentionally stronger** than faction starter artifacts — that's the incentive to race for them.

| Artifact | Trigger | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **Null Field** | Combat start, then every 8s (400 ticks) | Blocks enemy shield regeneration for 2s (100 ticks). Enemy shield HP is not wiped — only regen stops. |
| **Phase Drive** | Every 6s (300 ticks) | Opens a 1-second dodge window (50 ticks). Every incoming attack during the window has a 50% chance to be evaded entirely. |
| **Siege Battery** | Passive | All weapons deal +10% extra damage to hull. Shield damage is not boosted — the bonus only applies when damage reaches the hull. |
| **Repair Swarm** | Passive | Hull regenerates 0.2 HP per tick (1 HP every 5 ticks, ~10 HP/s). |
| **Chrono Capacitor** | Combat start | All weapon cooldowns halved (fire rate ×2) for the first 3 seconds (150 ticks). |
| **Vampire Array** | On each hit | Restores 15% of total damage dealt (shield + hull combined) as your own hull HP. |

Idryn Concord with **Concord Prism** amplifies all of the above by ×1.25 — this is why Idryn wants to collect as many Artifacts as possible.

## GETTING INTO A GAME

**Multiplayer only (2–6 players):**
1. One player clicks **HOST MULTIPLAYER** — a Room Code is shown.
2. Other players click **JOIN GAME** and enter the Room Code.
3. Every player picks a **Faction** (each faction is unique; one per room).
4. The host clicks **▶ START GAME** once everyone has picked.
5. If a player disconnects mid-game, they have **60 seconds to reconnect** before they are removed.

**Joining as Observer:**
- Any device can join a lobby and click **◉ JOIN AS OBSERVER** instead of picking a faction.
- The observer does not play — they have no faction, no turn, and no actions.
- Observers appear in the lobby list with a **◉ OBSERVER** badge.
- Multiple observers per room are allowed.
- The observer screen shows:
  - A full-screen live galaxy map with all commanders visible.
  - A **yellow rumor banner** (top-center) whenever an Artifact is rumored to appear in a system.
  - An **active commander indicator** (bottom-center) showing who is currently taking their turn.
  - **All combat** automatically — when any two commanders fight, the battle plays on the observer screen automatically. Player screens skip the combat animation when an observer is present.
  - The **win screen** at the end of the game.
- The observer screen is designed to be placed on a shared table display so all players can watch the map and battles without looking at their own devices.
